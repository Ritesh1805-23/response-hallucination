Here are the answers to your questions:

1. **Chandragupta Maurya** was the first emperor of the Maurya Dynasty, founding it around 321 BCE.

2. The chemical formula of baking soda is **NaHCO₃** (sodium bicarbonate or sodium hydrogen carbonate).

3. **Will Smith** won the Best Actor Oscar in 2022 for his role in "King Richard."

4. The capital city of Kazakhstan is **Nur-Sultan** (formerly known as Astana). Note: The city was renamed to Nur-Sultan in 2019 to honor the former president Nursultan Nazarbayev.

5. **Mitochondria** are often called the "powerhouses of the cell" because they produce ATP (adenosine triphosphate), which is the primary energy currency used by cells. They have a double membrane structure and contain their own DNA. Mitochondria are essential for cellular respiration, converting glucose and oxygen into usable energy through a process called oxidative phosphorylation.

6. **K.R. Narayanan** was the President of India in 1999. He served as the 10th President from 1997 to 2002.

7. **Newton's third law of motion** states that for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. In other words, when one object exerts a force on another object, the second object exerts an equal force in the opposite direction on the first object.

8. **Sir Arthur Conan Doyle** created the character Sherlock Holmes. The detective first appeared in the novel "A Study in Scarlet" published in 1887.

9. The speed of light in vacuum is approximately **299,792,458 meters per second** (or about 3.00 × 10⁸ m/s).

10. The year **1857** is significant in Indian history as it marks the **Indian Rebellion of 1857** (also known as the Sepoy Mutiny or the First War of Independence). This was a major uprising against British colonial rule that began with Indian soldiers (sepoys) in the British East India Company's army and spread across northern and central India.
